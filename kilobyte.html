What is Kilobyte? (#1)

For a start, a little background in computers!

Bit is the most basic unit that can be either 1 or 0.

A Byte is a unit of digital indormation that most commonly consists of 8 bits .

Kilobyte is the smallest unit of measurement greater than a byte . It precedes the Megabyte , which contains 1,000,000 bytes , while one kilobyte is technically 1,024 bit .

When do we use Kilobyte?


Kilobytes are often used to measure the size of small files. For example, a plain text document may contain 10 KB of data and therefore would have a file size of 10 kilobytes . Small website graphics are often between 5 KB and 100 KB in size.
